West Bengal Tour

The Cultural Hub of India

A slice of fertile land running down from the tea carpeted Himalayan foothills to sweltering mangroves of the Bay of Bengal; West Bengal is replete with allures of incredible destinations offering unique experiences. Hill stations, culture hotspots, heritage sites, ancient ruins, wildlife and rolling tea-hills- West Bengal is simply a bundle of joy!

Major Destinations in West Bengal

Kolkata- Interlacing the Traditional and the Modern
A cauldron of rich culture, history, literature and art; the first capital of colonial India & the current capital of West Bengal, the birthplace of Nobel Prize winner Rabindranath Tagore, and a foodie’s heaven? Welcome to Kolkata aka Calcutta, the "City of Joy".

Major Attractions

  • Howrah Bridge- The mascot of Kolkata
  • Victoria Memorial- Showcasing colonial memorabilia
  • Birla Temple- Dedicated to Lord Lakshmi Narayan
  • Marble Palace- Royal Palace showcasing Indian and European artwork
  • Indian Museum- the oldest and the largest museum in the country
  • Kali Ghat Temple- Dedicated to Goddess Kali- the revered one in West Bengal.
  • Botanical Gardens- Over 240-year-old gardens hosting a fascinating collection of plants.

Nearby Excursion of Kolkata

  • Shanti Niketan- Artistic and cultural hub of West Bengal

Sunderbans National Park- Call of the Royal Bengal Tiger
This tangle of mangrove jungles in the Bay of Bengal is a UNESCO-listed Heritage Site. Home to the ferocious Royal Bengal Tiger, rare crocodiles and hundreds of species of birds; a jungle boat safari in Sunderbans is an exhilarating experience.


Darjeeling ? of Teas and Monasteries
The gateway to the north-east, Darjeeling is home to exotic rhododendron forests, gorgeous Himalayan vistas, stunning monasteries, heritage toy train, bustling bazaars and lush tea-trails.

Major Attractions

  • Singalila National Park
  • Padmaja Naidu Himalayan Zoological Park
  • Tiger Hill
  • Observatory Hill & Mahakal Temple
  • Darjeeling Toy Train- A UNESCO World Heritage
  • Japanese Peace Pagoda

Kalimpong- Nature’s Bounty
Himalayan panorama, Buddhist monasteries, colonial bungalows, mesmerizing orchids, white water rafting and trekking- Kalimpong is a complete holiday package.

Major Attractions

  • Deolo Hill- Picturesque and offers adventure activities like horse riding, trekking and paragliding.
  • Kalimpong Cactus Nursery
  • Zong Dog Palri Fo Brang Gompa- Buddhist monastery
  • Crockety- A gorgeous colonial bungalow
  • River Rafting in Teesta
Get Free Tour QuotesView Tour Packages


Bishnupur-The Terracotta Temple Saga
Around 140kms from Kolkata, the 17th & 18th century Malla dynasty terracotta temples of Bishnupur are a blend of fusion architecture where curved Bengali roofs are topped with Islamic domes and arches.

Major Attractions

  • Rasmancha- Built by Malla kings for public celebration of festivals, especially Ras Festival
  • JorBangla Temple- Typical Bengali architecture with intricate carvings
  • Madan Mohan Temple- Built in the Ekaratna (single tower) style
  • Dalmadal Kaman Canon- the royal canon of the Malla Kings
  • Radhe Govind Temple- Dedicated to Lord Krishna and Radha

Gaur-Pandua Ruins- Timeless Glory
The twin towns, Gaur and Pandua once witness to three eras of glory ? the Buddhist Palas, the Hindu Sena Kings and the Muslim Sultan; are now home to some timeless ruins depicting the political and cultural dynamics of West Bengal.

Mandarmani Beach- Bay of Bengal at your Feet
Around 3kms from Digha, this quiet and secluded beach is a great place to relax and take long walks.

Fairs and Festivals of West Bengal

  • Durga Puja- Celebrating and venerating Goddess Durga and her many forms
  • Rash Mela- Dedicated to Lord Krishna and Radha
  • Poush Mela- Cultural festival in Shanti Niketan
  • Teesta Tea & Tourism Festival
  • Kolkata Rath Yatra- Chariot Festival
  • Gangasagar Mela- Celebrating Mother Ganges

Shopping in West Bengal
West Bengal is a delight for shoppers. Kantha, Tant & Baluchari sarees, milky white SolaPith artifacts, terracotta images & tiles, Dokra handicrafts, Kalighat Paintings, Putul (puppet) Dolls and Darjeeling Tea- be sure to take a slice of Bengal with you.

Food in West Bengal
Traditional Macher Jhol (fish curry), Kosha Mangsho (Mutton curry), Shukto (mixed vegetables), Chelo Kebabs, Puchkas (spicy water bombs), Kolkata Kathi Rolls, Momos, Gundruk, Roshogulla, Sandesh & Mishti Doi- West Bengal is a foodie’s paradise.

Best Time to Visit Kolkata
West Bengal is best visited in winters from October to March.

How to Reach
By Air- Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose International Airport at Dumdum, about 17kms from Kolkata, connects West Bengal with various Indian and international cities.
By Rail- All major cities in West Bengal are connected to the rest of the country by a network of direct & express trains.
By Road- West Bengal is connected with the rest of India by a network of well-maintained roads & highways.

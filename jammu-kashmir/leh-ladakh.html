Leh-Ladakh Tour

Get High on the Himalayas

Snow capped Himalayas, enchanting valleys, glacial landscape, precarious mountain passes, tumbling rivers and an unmatched spiritual bliss- Ladakh & Leh have always been high on the travel Itinerary of the bold and the fearless. Its barren cold beauty is a complete contrast to its warm hearted people, steaming hot cuisine and its hospitable culture- all borrowed from the neighboring state of Tibet.

Major Attractions of Leh- Ladakh

Monasteries- Spiritual Bliss
Perched high on the barren mountains, the monasteries of Leh-Ladakh, called Gompa in Tibetan, capture the essence of this Mini Tibet in India!? Let’s look at some of the beautiful monasteries here:

Hemis Gompa
This ancient monastery dates back to the 11th century but was later rebuilt in 1672 AD. The monastery receives royal patronage and is the most visited in the region, especially during the annual Hemis festival in June.

Spituk Gompa
Perched on a hillock, this late 14th century monastery is home to a golden-roofed shrine, ancient masks and arms. Its enormous statue of Mahakal is unveiled during the annual Spituk festival.

Lamayuru Monastery
This ancient monastery, the oldest & the largest in Ladakh, house around 150 monks. The legends and fables of Lamayuru are famous in the region. ?

Alchi Gompa, Shey Gompa, Thiksey Gompa, Stok Gompa and Phyang Gompa are some of the other revered monasteries of Leh-Ladakh.

Lakes of Leh-Ladakh
The spectacular panorama of Ladakh is dotted with beautiful lakes, both saline & fresh water.

Let's look at some of the most picturesque and popular lakes of Ladakh

Pangong Tso Lake
Pangong Tso, stretching from India to Tibet is known for its forever changing colors. The largest saline lake in Asia, Pangong Tso has forever been an allure for travelers in the region. Despite the saline content, the lake freezes in winters and the Ice Skating Festival is held here.

Tso Moriri
This glacial lake, perched high up on Changthang Plateau is one of the most beautiful sights in Leh-Ladakh. It can be reached only during summers and draws quite a crowd in those months.

Tso- Kar Lake
This beautiful saline lake is located on the Rupshu Plateau in Southern Ladakh. Its azure waters bordered by white salt crusted edges are a magical sight to behold.

Nayak Tso, Yarab Tso, Chagar Tso, Mirpal Tso and Kiagar Tso are some other picturesque lakes of Leh-Ladakh.

Adventure Activities

Motor Bike Expeditions, Khardungla
No biker can ever resist the thrill of riding the highest motorable road in the world, at 18380 feet, through the picturesque Khardungla Pass. Biking expeditions here are pretty much a norm.

Frozen River Trek
Zanskar River freezes in the winters and the adventurers walk across this blanket or chadar of ice on a trekking route called Chadar Trek. One of the most famous treks in India, it sure is a fascinating experience.

River Rafting
The gushing waters of Zanskar invite you to battle their fury on a raft. In summers, white-water rafting in Ladakh is quite popular amongst adventure enthusiasts.

Other Attractions in Leh- Ladakh

Shanti Stupa
This Japanese monument of peace near Leh is known for its architectural brilliance and its perfect views of Leh.

Leh Palace
This 16th century palace was once the world’s highest building. Its dark corridors and hidden passageways beckon you to explore further. Its beautiful murals and spiritual prayer room are simple stunning.

Nubra Valley
Often called the orchard of Ladakh, the green Nubra Valley stands distinctively from the stark landscape of Ladakh. When in Nubra, be sure to ride the double humped camel.

Magnetic Hill
This gravity defying hill is quite an attraction in the region. Well, science or surreal, the hill is quite a magnet for the travelers to Leh-Ladakh.

Nearby Excursion
Kargil- This wonderland in Suru Valley lies between mountain passes of Zozi La and Fotu La in Ladakh. Around 200kms from Leh, the bustling streets & bazaars of Kargil set amidst gorgeous scenery beckon you with open arms.

Shopping in Leh- Ladakh
Tibetan artifacts, Pashmina stoles, carpets & rugs and apricot jam & oil are some of the must-picked souvenirs from Ladakh. ?

Cuisine of Leh Ladakh
Influenced by Tibet, Ladakhi cuisine is quite flavorful. Nothing warms you like Thupka ? a noodle broth, or Tigmo- a spicy broth served with steamed bread. Be sure to try the salty Tibetan tea, Chhupri or Yak cheese and Chang-Tibetan rice beer.?

Best Time to Visit Leh-Ladakh
Leh-Ladakh is best visited in the months from May to September. However, January and February are best months for those looking forward to take the Frozen River Trek.

How to reach Leh- Ladakh
By Air- The domestic airport in Leh is connected with Indira Gandhi International Airport in Delhi.
By Rail- The nearest Railway Station is Jammu Tawi, around 700 kilometers from Ladakh.
By Road- Leh Srinagar Highway and Leh- Manali highway both take you to Ladakh. However, the roads run through treacherous mountain passes that are closed during winters.

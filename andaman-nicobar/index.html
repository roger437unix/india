Andaman & Nicobar Tour

Pearls in the Turquoise Waters

Shimmering cerulean waters flanked by dark forests, dazzling- white beaches, gorgeous sunsets and a pinch of history make the Andaman Islands a perfect Indian getaway. From sun-toasted beaches & colorful corals to impressive lighthouses & hiking trails and from historical edifices to snorkeling & scuba diving; this splendid archipelago off the Indian coast has delightful surprises tucked in its various nooks & crannies. Boat rides to its many tiny islands is an exhilarating experience.

Major Destinations

Port Blair
The capital of Andaman & Nicobar Islands, Port Blair is home to picturesque beaches humming with exciting beach activities.

Major Attractions

  • Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park
  • Wandoor Beach- Snorkeling, scuba diving and glass-bottom boat rides
  • Corbyn’s Cove- Jet skiing, speed boat riding
  • Mount Harriet- Hiking & trekking
Get Free Tour QuotesView Tour Packages


Ross Island (Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose Dweep)
The jungle clad colonial ruins of Ross Island, just a short boat ride from Port Blair give the feel of a lost city. Once known as the Paris of the east, the glorious ruins of Ross Island are sure to take you down the memory lane.

Major Attractions

  • Cellular Jail- Colonial Jail now converted to a museum. Light and Sound show here is phenomenal
  • Lighthouse
  • Japanese Bunkers- WWII military bunkers
  • Barren Island- Known for its active volcano
  • Forest Department Museum

North Bay Island
Just a little northward from Port Blair, North Bay is known for its stunning corals. Vibrant corals and colorful underwater life makes North Bay a wonderful place for snorkeling and scuba diving. Indulge in sea walking, jet skiing, speed boat riding and for a complete North Bay experience.

Havelock Island (Swaraj Dweep)
Sugar white beaches fringed by towering tropical trees and best diving waters of Havelock Island hold a magical appeal. Just one-and-a-half hour ferry ride from Port Blair, Havelock is a beach bum’s paradise.

Major Attractions

  • Radhanagar Beach- Swimming, snorkeling, scuba diving
  • Elephant Beach- snorkeling, scuba diving, kayaking, jet skiing, boating in glass bottom boats
  • Kalapathar Beach
  • Neil’s Cove

Fairs and Festivals in Andaman and Nicobar

  • Island Tourism Festival- Annual festival in January
  • Beach Festival- Annual festival in April
  • Music Festival / Monsoon Festival- Annual festival in August
  • Food Festival- Annual festival in September
  • Film Festival - Annual festival in November

Shopping in Andaman & Nicobar
Driftwood artifacts, pretty hats, wood carved furniture & panels, pearl jewelry, seashell accessories and handicrafts, bamboo bags & home d?cor stuff, coconut & coir crafts and spices are some of the souvenirs that you may pick from these beautiful islands.

Food in Andaman & Nicobar
Andaman Islands are simple seafood galore! Juicy lobsters, prawns, crabs, fried calamari, and many more seafood specialties are the gastronomical delights of Andaman.

Best Time to Visit
The islands are pleasurable in all seasons except monsoons. The peak season runs from October to May.

How to Reach
The islands can only be reached either by air or by sea.
By Air- Veer Savarkar International Airport in Port Blair connects the islands to the major cities in India.
By Sea- Ships from Vizag, Chennai and Kolkata sail regularly to Andaman & Nicobar Islands.

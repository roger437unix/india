Uttarakhand Tour

The Land of Mountains & Myths

Uttarakhand; revered as Dev Bhoomi (Land of Gods); cradles ancient mountains, peaks and holy lakes in its many folds. A land of diversities; from high-altitude pilgrimages to colonial era hill stations, from off-the-map villages to UNESCO heritage sites; from Hindu epics to the legends of Jim Corbett, from adrenalin pumping adventure to the tranquility of Yoga & meditation; this multifaceted northern Indian state has forever drawn travelers to its magical folds.

Major Destinations

Dehradun- An Array of Experiences
Perched delicately on the Himalayan foothills, this beautiful capital city of Uttarakhand is a heady concoction of colonial past, Tibetan culture and modern school education. Known for some remarkable institutions like the Doon School and Indian Military Academy; Dehradun is a major gateway to the hills of Uttarakhand.

Major Attractions

  • Forest Research Institute
  • Sahastradhara
  • Lacchiwalla
  • Tibetan Buddhist Temple
  • Robber’s Cave
  • Malsi Deer Park
  • Rajaji National Park

Haridwar- Gateway to Gods!
A small town; humming with the prayer chants and temple bells, where a dip in the Holy Ganges absolves you of your sins, where temple spires dominate the skyline and dhoti-clad priests are part of the landscape; this is Haridwar for you!

Major Attractions in Haridwar

  • Har Ki Pauri- Bathing Steps
  • Mansa Devi Temple
  • Shanti Kunj
  • Chandi Devi
  • Bharat Mata Temple
  • Ganga Aarti (River Ceremony)

Rishikesh- Spirituality Abounds
This self-styled “Yoga Capital of the World” where ancient practices like Ayurveda, Yoga & meditation rule the roost; is not only home to spiritual seekers but is also a popular hangout for backpackers and adventure enthusiasts.?

Major Attractions of Rishikesh

  • Ram Jhula
  • Lakshman Jhula
  • Neelkanth Mahadev Temple
  • Triyambakeshwar Temple
  • Triveni Ghat
  • White-water rafting, bungee jumping, camping, rock climbing, flying fox, kayaking, body surfing.

Auli- A Perfect Destination to See & Ski
A short Gondola styled cable-car ride from Joshimath takes you to Auli- the premier ski resort of Uttarakhand! Dotted with apple orchards, pine & oak trees and awesome views of Nanda Devi Peak, Auli is a fabulous destination in the hills of Uttarakhand?

Major Attractions of Auli

  • Auli Ski Resort
  • Auli Lake
  • Gurso Bugyal- Meadows near Auli
  • Chattrakund Lake

Nainital- Land of Lustrous Lakes
This quintessential colonial hill station is known for its many beautiful lakes, bustling Mall Road and picturesque spots.

Major Attractions in Nainital

  • Naini Lake & Naina Devi Temple
  • Naini Peak or China Peak
  • Tiffin Top
  • Bhimtal Lake
  • Sattal Lake

Mussoorie- Colonial Charm
This Raj-era hill station with its misty mountains, Tibetan settlements, and colonial architecture is a welcome break from the heat of the plains below.

Major Attractions in Mussoorie

  • Kempty Falls
  • Laal Tibba
  • Camel’s back
  • Gun Hill
  • Happy Valley

Other Destinations
Uttarakhand is home to many off beat destinations like Ranikhet, Dhanolti, Kausani and Binsar, to name a few. The exotic “Valley of Flowers”, a UNESCO-listed site, and various trekking trails in the Garhwal region of Uttarakhand are much sought after by backpackers and adventure enthusiasts.

Jim Corbett National Park
Sprawled over an area of 1318 sq.kms in the foothills of Kumaon, this park is home to the ferocious Royal Bengal Tiger! A rip-roaring experience, Jim Corbett is a perfect place for tiger safaris in India.

Pilgrimages in Uttarakhand
Though there are numerous pilgrimages in the state, the most scared is the Chota Char Dham Yatra comprising of remote hill destinations in Gangotri, Yamnotri, Badrinath, and Kedarnath.

Fairs & Festivals of Uttarakhand

  • Makar Sankranti
  • Ganga Dussehra
  • International Yoga Festival, Rishikesh
  • Nanda Devi Fair, Almora
  • Jauljibi & Thal Fairs
  • Uttarayani Fair, Bageshwar

Food in Uttarakhand
Kafuli, Bhange ki chutney, Gharwali Fannah, Phaanu, Chainasoo, Kumaoni Raita, Dubuk, Singauri and Baal Mithai are some of the local delicacies of the region.

Shopping In Uttarakhand
Local honey, jams, dry herbs, wooden artifacts, woolens, rugs, Kumauni jewelry & trinkets are some of the souvenirs you may collect from Uttarakhand.

Best Time to Visit
The ever-embracing state of Uttarakhand may be visited all over the year except during monsoon months of July & August, when the hill roads may be a bit treacherous.

How to Reach
By Air- Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun, caters to domestic air traffic.
By Rail- A good network of direct & express trains connects major cities in Uttarakhand to the rest of the country.
By Road- Uttarakhand is well connected with the major cities in the country with a network of roads and highway.

Rann of Kutch Festival Tour (Rann Utsav)

A Cultural Carnival

Layers of shimmering salt that dazzle diamond-like in sun & cast a silvery glow in moonlight, star-spangled skies and magical charms of the desert- We take you on Rann of Kutch Festival Tour in the colorful state of Gujarat. The scorching land of the Great Rann of Kutch creates an exuberance of colors, culture, and cuisine during this cultural extravaganza.

This cultural carnival, organized annually by Gujarat Tourism, celebrates the ethnicity and diversity of the region. It is the time when the wild white desert is tamed and transformed into a masterpiece.

Let?s take you on a quick walk through this gala packed with the kaleidoscopic splendor and heart-warming hospitality of the Rann.

Stay in the Tent City
The largest tent accommodation in India, with gleaming white luxury tents under the star-studded sky, beckons you for a delightful stay in the desert. Bedecked in vibrant Gujarati interiors and equipped with modern amenities, these tents are a perfect blend of rustic charm and urban facilities.

T?te-?-t?te with the Moon
Silvery gleaming desert under a blooming full moon is just like a poet?s verse- utterly romantic and simply magical! Go for a walk on the moonlit sands for a dreamy experience.

Cultural Gala
A riot of colors sprinkled over the white desert, the festival is ablaze with various folk dances, Sufi traditional music and folk dramas of Kutch.

Get Free Tour Quotes

Taste of Kutch
Try the mouthwatering Kutchi Dabeli, Kathiawari Thali, Chandrada Pakwaa, and Kharibaat and wash it all down with a glass of spiced buttermilk. For those with a sweet tooth, there are piping hot jalebis and dudhpak to complete the meal.

Rustic Desert Safaris
Romantic camel safaris in the moonlit desert tinged with silvery hues, and rustic nomad-like camel cart rides evoke the charm of this gorgeous salt desert.

Adventure in Rann of Kutch Festival
Rann is as adventurous as it is romantic. Go for solo drives on Desert Patrol Vehicles, embrace the skies on a hot air balloon ride, go para-motoring on a powered glider and try your hand at archery and rifle shooting, for some thrill in the Rann of Kutch Festival.

Shopping in Rann of Kutch Festival
Vividly colored block printed fabrics, beadwork bags, mirror-work d?cor, Banni & Rabari embroidered items, Rogan artwork, and Kutchi mud paintings- Kutch?s rich handicrafts entice you from many stalls at the fair. Take along a slice of Kutch with you in the form of handcrafted camel leather items, splendid silver jewelry and Kutch handloom fabrics.

Best Time to Visit
Rann of Kutch Festival, the longest fest in India, starts in November and continues till mid February. However, the best time to visit the festival is on full moon nights.

How to Reach
The Festival takes place in Dhordo Village, situated on the edge of the Rann of Kutch.
By Air- Bhuj Airport, around 77kms from Dhordo Village connects the region with some of the major Indian cities.
By Rail- The railhead at Bhuj, 77kms away, is the closest railway station.
By Road- An excellent network of roads and highways connect Dhordo Village with other cities in the region.

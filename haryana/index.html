Haryana

A Blend of History, Mythology and Technology

Carved out of East Punjab in 1966, this tiny state set in the Aravalli foothills boasts of long mythological and historical connections. The site for the battle in Mahabharata, the Hindu epic; the venue for the famous battles of Panipat that paved the way for the Mughal Empire in India and the home to major centers of rebellion during the Indian freedom struggle; Haryana is now a prosperous state with vast lush expanses, ancient temples and pretty lakes.

Major Destinations

Chandigarh- The Rocking Capital
The joint capital of Punjab & Haryana, the planned city of Chandigarh is home to artistic edifices and modern structures.

Major Attractions

  • Leisure Valley
  • Rock Garden
  • Government Museum and Art Gallery
  • Sukhna Lake
  • Shanti Kunj
  • Chhatbir Zoo

Morni Hills- View from the Aravallis
Around 40kms from Chandigarh, the sprawling Morni Hills are simply a nature’s wonder. Picturesque and pristine, the hills are a perfect place for family picnics.

Major Attractions

  • Tikkar Lake- Boating
  • Morni Fort

Kurukshetra- Of Myths and Wonders
Believed to be the battlegrounds in the epic battle of the Mahabharata and the sacred spot where Lord Krishna preached Bhagvad Gita to Arjuna; Kurukshetra is now a renowned pilgrimage.

Major Attractions

  • Brahma Sarovar
  • Sannihit Sarovar
  • Sheikh Chehlli Tomb
  • Dharohar Haryana Museum

Panipat- Mughal's Glory Ground
The historical site of the battles that paved way for the Mughal Empire in India, Panipat, is also known as the “City of Weavers” and is world famous for its handloom products.

Major Attractions

  • Tomb of Ibrahim Lodhi
  • Tomb of Bu-Ali Shah Qalandar, a Sufi Saint
  • Devi Temple
  • Kabuli Bagh Mosque
  • The Panipat Museum

Gurugram- The Hi-Tech City
From the mythological pages of Mahabharata to the hi-tech wonder, Gurugram has emerged as the corporate hub of Haryana.

Major Attractions

  • Sultanpur National Park & Bird Sanctuary
  • Kingdom of Dreams
  • Damdama Lake
  • Farrukh Nagar Fort

Faridabad- Industrial Hub of Haryana
This industrial city established in 1607 AD during the Mughal rule is not only the commercial core of Haryana but also a great tourist spot.

Major Attractions

  • Badkhal Lake
  • Shirdi Sai Temple
  • Raja Nahar Singh’s Palace
  • Surajkund Lake
  • Tomb of Farid Khanb- a Sufi saint

Fairs and Festivals of Haryana

  • Lohri- Harvest Festival
  • Teej- Monsoon Festival
  • Mahabharata Festival, Kurukshetrs
  • Surajkund Craft Festival
  • Sohna Car rally
  • Pinjore Heritage Festival

Shopping in Haryana
Exquisite pottery, pretty handloom fabric, woven cane furniture, rugs & upholstery fabric, tilla juttis (footwear), and Phulkari embroidered fabrics make must buy memorabilia in Haryana.

Food in Haryana
The local food of Haryana is quite simple, wholesome and delicious with emphasis on milk and milk products like ghee (rarified butter) and fresh homemade butter. However, the cities of Haryana, especially Chandigarh and Gurugram offer an array of cuisine from all over the world.

Best Time to Visit Haryana
Haryana is best visited in the winter months from October to March.

How to Reach
By Air- Chandigarh International Airport caters to domestic and selective international air traffic.
By Rail- Most of the cities in Haryana are connected to major Indian cities by a network of Indian Railways.
By Road- An excellent network of roads & highways connect Haryana Punjab to the rest of India.

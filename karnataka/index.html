Karnataka Tour

A Heady Brew of Old and New

Oscillating between the old world charm and the urban glitter; this fascinating southern state of India is like a pendulum that sways from the ruins of Hampi and opulence of Mysore to the skyscrapers and glitzy pubs of its lively capital, Bengaluru. Its charisma extends to the lush hills resplendent with emerald coffee and spice plantations, rich wildlife reserves and a magical shoreline.

Major Destinations

Bengaluru- The Urban Cool
Earlier known as Bangalore, this tech capital of India serves as the modern capital of Karnataka. Legendary for its information technology industry, brewing start-ups, craft beer, standup comedians and a buzzing nightlife; Bengaluru is the face of modern India.

Major Attractions

  • Ulsoor Lake
  • Lal Bagh Botanical Garden
  • Bengaluru Palace
  • Tipu Sultan’s Palace & Fort
  • Lal Bagh Botanical Garden

Hampi- A Marvel of Architecture
The magnificent ruins of Hampi are silent witness to the glorious days of the Vijayanagar Empire in the Deccan. Built between 14th and 16th century AD, these relics depict both the creative genius of mankind and his capacity for destruction.

Major Attractions

  • Virupaksha Temple
  • Sasivekalu Ganesha
  • Kadavekalu Ganesha
  • Hemakuta Hill Temple
  • Hampi Bazaar

Extension Tours from Hampi

Badami- A UNESCO Heritage Site, around 140 kms from Hampi; Badami is home to beautiful cave temples.
Hassan- Located around 335kms from Hampi; Hassan is dotted with spectacular temples and forts.

Mysure- Palace Galore!
A grand city of palaces, gardens, lakes, silks and sandalwood; Mysore is sure to dazzle you with its historical, cultural and architectural riches.

Major Attractions

  • Mysore Palace
  • Lalitha Maha
  • Jagmohan Palace
  • Karanji Lake
  • St Philomena Church
  • Chamundeshwari Temple
  • Brindavan Gardens.

Fairs and Festivals of Karnataka

  • Hampi Festival- 3 day Tourism Festival celebrated in the month of January.
  • Kambala Festival- Buffalo Race
  • Ugadi- Karnataka New year celebrated in the month of March or April
  • Rajyotsava Day- Karnataka’s birthday celebrated on 1st November
  • Dussehra- 10 day festival in Mysore in October
  • Pattadakal Dance Festival- Celebrated in the month of January

Shopping in Karnataka
Sandalwood carvings, rosewood inlay, channapatna lacquer ware & toys, Mysore traditional paintings, Bidriware artifacts, Navalgund rugs, stone carvings, wood carvings, traditional jewelry, mirror and embroidery work, terracotta art and Mysore silk sarees are some of the handcrafted specialties of Karnataka.

Food in Karnataka
Udipi Masala Dosa, Mangalore fish, Pandi (pork) curry, Kudumbattu (rice dumplings), Kori Gassi (tangy chicken curry), Neer Dosa, Kane Reva Fry (fried fish) and Mysore Pak (pudding) are some of the culinary gems of Karnataka.

Best time to Visit
Hill stations like Coorg are great getaways in summers but due to the hot Deccan Plateau, most of the Karnataka is best visited from October to March.

How to Reach
By Air- Kempegowda International Airport, Bengaluru, around 40kms from the main city, connects Karnataka with major cities across the globe.
By Rail- Karnataka is well- connected with the rest of the country by an extensive network of Indian Railways.
By Road- A well-maintained network of roads and highways connect Karnataka to major cities in India.

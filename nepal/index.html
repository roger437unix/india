Nepal Tour

Realm of the Mountains

Snuggling cozily in the lap of the Himalayas, Nepal is an incredible concoction of mesmerizing nature, ancient culture and adrenalin pumping adventure. Whether you a nature enthusiast, an adventure addict, a pilgrimage, a wildlife aficionado or a history buff; Nepal is sure to entice you with its plethora of attractions.?

The History
Ruled by various dynasties, from the Gopalas to the Kirtas and from the Licchavis to the Mallas; Nepal was finally unified by Gorkhas under the Shah dynasty who reigned supreme for around 150 years. The Shah dynasty was overthrown by the Maoist rebels and Nepal was declared a democracy in May 2008.

The Culture
A mishmash of Buddhism & Hinduism; Nepal’s rich cultural legacy is deep rooted in its religion, family life, and its colorful fairs & festivals. Home to many UNESCO Preserved World Heritage Sites; Nepal comes alive in its medieval cities, ancient stupas, golden-roofed temples and sacred mountains.

Major Destinations

Kathmandu- The Political and Cultural Capital of Nepal
A chaotic combination of sounds, sight and smell; this intoxicating capital of Nepal is blessed by the living Goddess Kumari and protected by Lord Pashupatinath. Born from ancient history, diverse culture & ethnicity and marvelous architecture, Kathmandu is a fine specimen of ancient heritage juxtaposed with modern times.

Major Attractions of Kathmandu

  • Pashupatinath Temple- Dedicated to Lord Shiva, the temples complex consists of over 500 smaller shrines.
  • Hanuman Dhoka- the Royal Palace complex built by Licchavis and expanded by other rulers.
  • Itum Bahal- A Buddhist Monastery
  • Boudhanath Stupa- Giant stupa decked with giant Buddha eyes surveying the city.
  • Swayambhunath Temple- Ancient hilltop temple also called the Monkey Temple
  • Kathmandu Durbar Square- A UNESCO-listed site housing courtyards, quadrangles and temples.

Pokhara- The Himalayan Beauty
Towering mountains, shimmering lakes, spectacular scenery and hear-pumping adventure activities define the beauty city of Pokhara.

Major Attractions of Pokhara

  • Devi Falls
  • Varahi Templep- Dedicated to Lord Vishnu
  • Japanese Peace Pagoda
  • Phew Tal- The second largest lake in Nepal
  • International Mountain Museum- Dedicated to the mountains of Nepal

Bhaktapur- The Divine City
Once the epicenter of the Kathmandu Valley, Bhaktapur is home to incredible monuments of the medieval era. Towering pagoda style temples, colossal royal courtyards and stunning palaces mark this medieval city in Nepal.

Major Attractions of Bhaktapur

  • Bhairabnath Temple
  • 55 Window Palace
  • Dattareya Temple
  • National Art Gallery
  • Char Dham Temples

Lalitpur /Patan- Basking in the Glorious Past
Historically known as Patan, Lalitpur was one of the three royal cities of Nepal. Renowned for its Durbar Square, exquisite temples and Ashokan Stupas, Lalitpur is also an adventure travelers’ haven.

Major Attractions of Lalitpur /Patan

  • Durbar Square- the ancient Royal Palace
  • Patan Gate
  • Taleju Bell
  • Baha Monastery
  • Trekking, mountain biking, white water rafting and nature walks

Other Destinations in Nepal

  • Nagarkot- A beautiful hill station in the Himalayas
  • Annapurna Circuit- A trekking circuit
  • Janakpur- The city of ponds, a Hindu pilgrimage
  • Lumbini- Birthplace of Lord Buddha, a Buddhist pilgrimage

Wildlife in Nepal
Nepal’s diverse landscape ranging from snowcapped Himalayas to foothill grasslands and Rara Lake eco region are home to a variety of flora & fauna. These two parks have been declared UNESCO Natural World Heritage sites:

Sagarmatha National Park- Home to Mt. Everest, the highest peak in the world (8848m)

Chitwan National Park- Jungle safaris

Fairs and Festivals of Nepal

  • Buddha Jayanti
  • Losar- New Year
  • Maha Shivratri- Dedicated to Lord Shiva
  • Ghode Jatra- Annual Horse Parade
  • Dashain- Dedicated to Goddess Durga
  • Tihar- Annual festival of Lights

Shopping in Nepal
Thangkas (Buddhist paintings), Rice paper diaries & journals, vivid masks & puppets, traditional jewelry, hemp bags, Buddhist singing bowls and Nepali shawls & caps are some of must buy memorabilia in Nepal.

Cuisine in Nepal
The staple food of Nepal is dal-bhat-tarkari which means lentils-rice- vegetables. Gundruk, Sel Roti, Chatmari, Yomari, momos and Jaju Dhau, are some of the crowning dishes of Nepali cuisine.

Varanasi Tour

Sacred, Spiritual and Soulful

One of the world’s oldest continually inhabited cities with over 2000 years of history in its wake, Varanasi, often known as Kashi or Benaras, is a timeless city of historical relics and spiritual bliss. Its ancient ghats and labyrinths of narrow alleyways; alive with incense fragrance, jingling temple bells & echoing chants, and tempting aromas of street food; are a pleasure for senses. Considered as the abode of Lord Shiva, Varanasi offers you glimpses of ancient rituals, customs and culture. The glorious sunrise on the Ganges, the hustle-bustle of life and the harsh reality & rituals of death; Varanasi is not just a city but a powerful experience.

Major Attractions

The Ganges
Life in Varanasi revolves around the sacred Ganges. A holy dip in the Ganges starts the day that ends with the evening worship at the river. ?Stroll along its numerous ghats (riverfront steps), watch the innumerable rituals on the ghats or just take a boat ride on the river to catch a spectacular sunrise or sunset.

Vishwanath Temple
The gold plated dome & tower of Kashi Vishwanath radiates faith & spirituality. Dedicated to Lord Shiva as Vishveswara- the lord of the universe, the temple is one of the 12 Jyotirlingas in the country. Plundered & destroyed numerous times in the past by invaders & rulers alike, the current structure built by Ahilya Bai of Indore dates back to 1776 AD.

Assi Ghat
Assi Ghat, one of the ancient & the most famous ghats of the city, is the place where the River Assi merges with the holy Ganges. Shiva devotees take a dip in the waters here before praying to Lord Shiva.

Dashashwamedh Ghat
This colorful & lively ghat takes its name from the mythical legend that Lord Brahma sacrificed (medh) 10 (dash) horses (ashwa) on this ghat to welcome Lord Shiva. Be sure to witness the grandeur of the evening Ganga Aarti (river worship) at Dashashwamedh Ghat. The ethereal blaze of the lamps, the strong incense and the melodious chants; the ritual simply mesmerizes the senses.

Manikarnika Ghat
The final destination for many Hindus, this is the main ghat for cremation. All day long, bodies are cremated on top of pyres on the riverfront steps. The entire ritual is a compelling spectacle.

Shopping in Varanasi
The famous Benarasi silk saris with elaborate zari work are the insignia of Varanasi. “Gulabi Minakari” or pink enameled jewelry & ornaments, stone carved curios, Rudraksha beads & wooden toys are also popular souvenirs of the city.

Food in Varanasi
Crispy Kachoris & Sabzi, Chena Dahi Vada, tomato Chat, Lal Peda and sweet Lassi are some of the signature dishes of Varanasi.

Best Time to visit Varanasi
Best time to visit Varanasi is in the winters from October to March.

How to visit Varanasi
By Air- Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport, Varanasi caters to both domestic and international traffic.
By Rail- Varanasi is connected with all major cities in the country with a network of direct and express trains.
By Road- A well-maintained network of highways & roads connects Varanasi to all major cities in the country.

Kollam Tour

Portal to the Backwaters of Kerala

A fascinating port city with attractive beaches, soulful temples, tranquil backwaters, and old fishing harbors; Kollam is a much sought honeymoon and holiday destination in Kerala. Often known by its anglicized name Quilon, Kollam was once a commercial hub for spice & cashew traders on the ancient spice route in the Arabian Sea. The surrounding waterways of Ashtamudi Lake completely transform its chaotic scenery into a soul soothing experience. Our tour packages take you through the boisterous charm of Kollam to the serenity of its backwaters.

Major Attractions of Kollam

Kollam Beach
The main beach, around 2kms from the city, is a delight to watch at sunrise and sunset when the waters and the horizon are awash in shades of vermillion. Once an active harbor for Chinese trade ships, you may still see remnants of Chinese fishing nets, Chinese water pots and sampan-like boats on this beach. A chaotic fish market at its north end adds local color to the beach.

Backwaters of Ashtamudi Lake
The lake derives its name from its 8 arm-like channels. Considered a major gateway to the backwaters, Ashtamudi Lake is famous for its houseboat rides on the scenic route from Kollam to Alleppey. Its lush surroundings, clear waters and exotic biodiversity are simply enchanting.

Sasathamkotta Lake
This beautiful lake, flanked by lush hills, paints a clean, green and serene picture on the canvass of Kollam. Named after the ancient Sastha Temple located on its bank, this beautiful lake is a Ramsar Convention designated wetland for its unique biodiversity.

Thangassery Lighthouse
This 144ft lighthouse was built in 1902 and its beacon still shows way to the ships up to 13 miles at sea. Climb up for a bird?s eye view of its pretty surroundings.

Thevally Palace
Built around 1819, on the banks of Ashtamudi Lake, this palace is a striking blend of Portuguese, Dutch and British architecture. An epicenter of activities in the Travancore Kingdom, Thevally Palace now houses government offices.

Munroe Island
Named after an Englishman, Resident Colonel John Munroe of Travancore, Munroe Island is a cluster of 8 islands separated by channels & lakes. Its narrow waterways, pretty lakes and canal cruises offer a pleasant ambience.

Ayurveda in Kollam
A renowned Ayurveda destination in Kerala, Kollam is known for its rejuvenating and holistic healing practices. Unwind your city self in an Ayurvedic spa that is sure to delight you with its luxurious hospitality and revitalizing care. Rejuvenate your body, mind and spirit with the healing touch of Ayurveda in Kollam.

Shopping in Kollam
Kollam?s many local markets are a shopper?s delight. Besides colorful textiles, glittering jewelry, and fancy trinkets from Kerala; Kollam is the place to buy cashews and coir based products.

How to Reach
By Air- Trivandrum International Airport around 66kms from Kollam, is the closest airport.
By Rail- Kollam Junction connects Kollam with the major cities in India through a network of direct & express trains.
By Road- A well-maintained network of roads & highways link Kollam to all major Indian cities.

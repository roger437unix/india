Kerala Tour

A Tropical Symphony

God’s Own Country, Kerala, is a verdant wonderland where you can cruise the tranquil backwaters, walk the tea clad hill slopes, hike up the craggy Western Ghats, and renew your body & soul with its ancient Ayurvedic treatments. Home to many vibrant & ancient traditions such as Kathakali (dance form), Theyyam (ritualistic worship), Kalaripayattu (martial art) and Snake-boat races; Kerala depicts nature & culture at its exotic best.

Major Destinations of Kerala

Thiruvananthapuram / Trivandrum- The Charming Capital of Kerala
Time stands still amidst lush environs, beautiful temples and Ayurvedic traditions of Trivandrum. This beautiful capital city still preserves its old world charm and simplicity.

Major Attractions of Trivandrum

  • Padmanabhaswamy Temple
  • Kuthiramalika Palace & Museum
  • Natural History Museum
  • Samudra Beach
  • Puttan Malika Palace

Kochi- The Cultural Capital of Kerala
Kochi is a melting pot of numerous cultures & traditions. Its classic Dutch architecture, Chinese fishing nets, and traditional spice markets are sure to delight you.

Major Attractions of Kochi

  • Mattancherry Palace or Dutch Palace
  • Jew Town
  • St. Francis’s Church
  • Kerala Folklore Theatre & museum
  • Chinese Fishing Nets

Alleppey/Alappuzha- The Tranquil Backwaters
Around 70kms from Kochi, Alleppey is home to the beautiful backwaters of Kerala. An intricate maze of interconnected lagoons, canals, lakes and inlets; these backwaters are home to an incredible collection of flora and fauna.

Major Attractions of Alleppey

  • Houseboat Cruise- Stay in the houseboats, try fresh toddy & Kerala cuisine and laze around as the boat moves along the scenic villages.
  • Alleppy Beach
  • Krishnapuram Palace
  • Marari Beach

The other popular backwaters in Kerala are in Kochi, Kumarakom and Kollam.

Munnar ? The Cozy Hill Retreat
Located in the gently rolling Sahyadri Range or the Western Ghats, Munnar is the glorious hill retreat in Kerala. Lush green tea slopes, majestic mountains, shimmering lakes and tumbling waterfalls are the essence of this pristine hill destination in Kerala.

Major Attractions of Munnar

  • Attukal Waterfalls
  • Chithirapuram
  • Photo Point
  • CSI Christ Church

Ponmudi, Idukki and Wayanad are some of the other beautiful hill stations of Kerala.

Kovalam- Call of the Golden Sands
This pristine beach town near Thiruvananthapuram is home to expansive beaches with rolling surf, swaying palms and luxurious beach resorts where fresh sea food and Ayurvedic massage help you relax and rejuvenate.

Varkala, Marari and Kannur are some of the other golden beaches of Kerala.

Major Attractions of Kovalam

  • Lighthouse beach
  • Veli Tourist Village
  • Vellayani Lake
  • Hawa Beach
  • Vizhinjam Marine Aquarium
  • Kovalam Beach

Wildlife in Kerala
Nestled in the forested depths of the majestic Western Ghats are National Parks of Kerala that are home to the protected and endangered species like the Indian Sloth-Bear, Lion-Tailed Macaque, Indian Bison, Bengal Tiger, Asian Elephants and? Nilgiri Ibex. The major wildlife sanctuaries of Kerala are:

  • Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary,
  • Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary,
  • Wayanad Sanctuary
  • Eravikulam National Park

Yoga & Ayurvedic Practices in Kerala
Known as the Ayurveda Capital of India, Kerala offers a complete 5 point treatment for detoxification & rejuvenation along with yoga and meditation practices for revitalization of the body & the soul.

Fairs & Festivals of Kerala

  • Onam- Snake Boat race
  • Attukal Pongala- Temple festival
  • Thrissur Pooram- Bedecked procession of elephants & drummers.?
  • Nehru Trophy Boat race

Cuisine of Kerala
Kerala cuisine is a blend of fragrant spices and soothing flavors of coconut. Puttu & Kadala curry, Appam “(hopper) & Stew, idiyappams (string hoppers), Chicken fry, Prawn Curry, Naadan Beef Fry and Malabar Parota are some of the delectable dishes of Kerala.

Shopping in Kerala
Munnar tea & coffee, spices, banana chips, coconut & coir products, aromatic oils, wooden souvenirs & collectibles are some things that you should take back as memorabilia from Kerala.

Best Time to visit Kerala
Peak season for visiting Kerala is from September to March followed by monsoon months from June to August.

How to reach Kerala
By Air- Kochi International Airport is well connected to major cities in the world.

By Rail-Most cities in Kerala are connected with the rest of the country by a network of Indian Railways.

By Road- Kerala is easily accessible from the rest of the country by a well maintained network of roads & highways.

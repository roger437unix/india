Munnar

A Thousand Shades of Green!

This gorgeous hill station in the Nilgiris where misty veils cover the mountains, high peaks frolic with cloudy wreaths and emerald carpets of tea roll over the gentle hill- Munnar’s exhilarating beauty is simply unparalleled. An erstwhile British summer resort, Munnar with its ambient climate through the year, is still a choicest destination in Kerala. Its flavorful tea and aromatic spices add to its exotic charm.

Major Attractions in Munnar

Attukal Waterfalls
Around 9kms from Munnar, a winding road leads you to Attukal Waterfalls with set spectacularly with lush hills and thick forest in the backdrop. A favorite spot for picnics and romantic strolls, Attukal is a hotspot on Munnar itinerary.

CSI Christ Church
This colonial, Neo-Gothic church was constructed in 1911 with granite imported from England. Set amidst serene hills, this church is known for its elegant interiors and exquisite stained glass windows.

Photo Point
Painted in vivid hues of nature with midnight blue mountains, shamrock hills, silver towering oaks and tiny rivulets meandering across the landscape; this aptly named spot is a photographer’s dream. Let your camera do the talking!

Lockhart Gap
Scenic wild trails in dense forests, exotic flowers, mist-layered landscape and stunning views of valleys and plantations sprawled at your feet- pack your picnic basket and head to Lockhart Gap for a delightful day amidst nature.

Top Station
Located near Kerala-Tamil Nadu border, around 30kms from Munnar, this is the realm of lofty mountains wreathed in cloudy spirals. You just can’t miss the gorgeous valley views from this spot. Every 12 years, Neelakurinji flower blooms cover the hill and awash it in shades of blue.

Kanan Devan Tea Museum
One of its kinds in India, this museum tells you all about the tea history of a century old tea plantation that developed from a basic tea roller to an automated factory. Besides showcasing a lot of antique equipment, the museum offers you a tour of the plantation & tea factory and holds tea tasting sessions.

Kundala Dam Lake
This shimmering lake around 21kms from Munnar is famous for its Shikara rides. Flanked by lush hills and breathtaking scenery, Shikara rides here are reminiscent of Dal Lake in Kashmir.

Shopping in Munnar
Take home an overwhelming variety & flavors of tea, traditional spices and aromatic oils from the local bazaars and tea estates of Munnar.

Best Time to Visit
Munnar’s pleasant climate makes it a year-round destination. Though the tourist season runs from October to May, monsoon season in Munnar from June to September has its own charm as the mountains come alive with lush green foliage and waterfalls cascade down in full glory.

How to Reach
By Air- The closest airport in Cochin, around 110kms from Munnar, connects it to major international cities.
By Rail- Aluva Railway Station, around 110kms from Munnar, is the closest railhead connecting Munnar to other cities in the region.
By Road- Munnar is well-connected to the cities in the area with a good network of roads and highways taking you on a scenic drive to Munnar.

Meghalaya

The Realm of the Clouds

Cool mountains, pine scented forests, fast flowing rivers, cascading waterfalls and astounding living bridges set the tone for this incredibly beautiful state amidst the seven sisters of the northeast. Though mainly a Christian state; Meghalaya’s ethnic tribes- Khasi, Jaintia and Garo; define its vibrant culture and ancient traditions. Known for the wettest place on earth, cleanest village in Asia, crystal river and double-decker living root bridge; Meghalaya is certainly a place of incredible natural beauty and ingenuity.

Major Destinations

Shillong- the Eastern Scotland
Shillong takes its name from the word “Leishyllong”, meaning God or Superpower who is believed to reside on the Shillong Peak and shower his blessings on the city. Well, it also goes by the moniker-“Scotland of the East” for its lush hills, colonial churches and cobbled street that gave the homesick English a semblance of the Scottish highlands.

Major Attractions

  • Umiam Lake
  • Shillong Peak
  • Lady Hydri Park & Zoo
  • Elephant Falls
  • The Sacred Forest of Mawphlang
  • Ward’s Lake
  • Don Bosco Center for Indigenous Culture
  • Cathedral Catholic Church

Cheerapunji/Sohra- Where Monsoon Rules!
One of the wettest places on earth, Cheerapunji, perched high on the fringes of the Khasi Hills is a mesmerizing town where shades of green rule the beautiful landscape. Thick rainforests, ribbons of waterfalls, dramatic hills overlooking the plains of Bangladesh and the colorful traditions of the Khasi people make Cheerapunji a beautiful destination.

Major Attractions

  • Double Decker Living Root Bridge- A 150- year-old manmade wonder
  • Nohkalikai Falls
  • Mausmai Caves
  • The Seven Sisters Falls
  • Thangkharang Park
  • Dainthlen Falls

Dawki- Crystals Waters at the Indo-Bangla Border
Tucked away between the hillocks near the Indo-Bangladesh Border, this the quaint village of Dawki is known for its emerald glass-like River Umngot where you can see the colorful fish and glistening rocks at the bottom of the crystal-clean river.

Major Attraction

  • Boating in the glass-like River Umngot

Mawlynnong Village- Clean & Green!
The people of Mawlynnong work hard to upkeep this cleanest village in Asia. Not a leaf or twig is out of place here! A great change from the plastic strewn cities, Mawlynnong with its pretty flowers, manicured grass and high view-points offers spectacular views of the sprawling plains of Bangladesh.

Fairs & Festivals of Meghalaya

  • Autumn Festival- A Tourism Festival in Shillong
  • Nongkrem Festival- Khasi Harvest Festival
  • Bacardi NH7 Weekender- Rock Music Festival, Shillong
  • Behdeinkhlam Festival- Jaintia festival in July before sowing seeds.
  • Wangala Festival- Garo festival of Thanksgiving.
  • Strawberry Festival, Sohliya

Shopping in Meghalaya
Hand woven shawls, stoles & warm clothing, Scottish dress material, Khasi organic scrubs, Sohiong (local fruit wine), bamboo & cane artifacts and fish & pork pickles- Meghalaya offers you amazing takeaways!

Food in Shillong
Meghalaya is non vegetarian’s paradise- especially if you love pork. When in Mehalaya, be sure to try Ja doh (rice cooked in pork or chicken stock), Doh Kleh- pork & onions, Doh Shiar Ngiong & DohNngiong (meat curries with black sesame flavor) and Doh kha sdieh (fried fish).

Best Time to Visit
Meghalaya’s ambient climate calls for year-round travel. However, the peak travel season to this charming state is from February to May and then from October to December.

How to Reach Meghalaya
By Air- Umroi Airport, around 25kms from Shillong, caters to domestic air traffic.
By Rail- Guwahati, the closest railhead around 100kms away, is connected with all major Indian cities by a good rail network.
By Road- Most of the cities in Meghalaya are well connected by a good network of roads & highways.
